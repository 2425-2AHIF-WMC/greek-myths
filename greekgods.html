The ancient greeks had a polytheistic religion, where every god and goddess had certain areas they were responsible for. Therefore, it was very important to whom you prayed for what occasion. The most important of the gods were the 12 Olympians, which were more powerful than any other gods.

Hestia

Hestia was the first child of the titan Kronos, who ruled the world before the gods. Kronos was a tyrant and scared to lose his position, so he swallowed her whole. She stayed in his stomach until Zeus freed her and her siblings. Hestia is the goddess of the hearth and the home. She doesn’t really have temples as she was worshipped in every home. She used to be an Olympian until she gave up her throne for Dionysus.

Demeter

Demeter is the second daughter of Kronos and had the same beginning as Hestia. Afterwards she became the goddess of crops, grains, food and fertility of the earth. She had a child with Zeus and Poseidon respectively and swore to never marry after that. Instead, she devoted her time to her daughter Persephone.

Hera

Hera is the last daughter of Kronos and was swallowed as well. After Zeus saved her, she married him and became queen of the gods. Furthermore, she’s the goddess of marriage, women and families, which is why she is always faithful to Zeus and vengeful towards his mistresses.

Hades

Hades is the oldest son of Kronos, although often mistaken as the youngest. He is the god of the underworlds and the dead. Through him the underworld gained order and every soul got the afterlife they deserved. Because he lived in the underworld and not on Mount Olypmus he is not considered one of the Olympians.

Poseidon

Poseidon is the second son of Kronos and the last child to be swallowed. He is the of the seas and earthquakes. He married a nymph and had the most romances out of all the gods. Not once but twice he had a horse as a child, one of which was Pegasus. He was also turned mortal once for insulting Zeus.

Zeus

Zeus is the last child of Kronos and was hidden from him to later save his siblings and defeat his father. He is the king of the gods and god of the sky. The only one above him is fate. He had three children with Hera but only Ares is well known. Zeus also had a lot of romances that led to children which always evoked the wrath of Hera.

Aphrodite

Aphrodite is possibly the oldest goddess and wasn’t born but came into existence through a mix of sea foam and blood of a titan. She’s the goddess of love and beauty and can be both calm and furious quickly. Her husband is Hephaistos, as Zeus ordered, but she does not love him but is with Ares instead most of the time.

Ares

Ares is the firstborn child of Hera and Zeus and the god of war and courage. He represents both the bravery and bloody brutality necessary in war. He dates Aphrodite behind Hephaistos‘ back but otherwise does not have many partners. As a more savage god he is often feared, however he is also a known protector of women.

Hephaistos

Hephaistos is the god of fire, blacksmiths and anything to do with craftwork. He is Heras son, with no father, who she had to prove to Zeus she didn’t need him. As a result Hephaistos was crippled and ugly, so he was thrown off Olympus, and he stayed in exile for a while until he was allowed to return.

Athena

Athena wasn’t born but instead jumped out of Zeus’ head after he complained about a bad headache and Hephaistos pried his skull open. She's the goddess of strategic war, military strategy and wisdom, which sets her in contrast to her brother Ares, and most often represented with an owl. Poseidon and her have gotten in a few fights and their relationship is strained.

Apollo & Artemis

Artemis and Apollo are twin gods born to a titan and Zeus, which enraged Hera, and she forbade any land to let their mother give birth. In the end she found refuge on a floating island. Artemis is the goddess of the hunt, wilderness, childbirth, chastity and the moon. She also has a group of nymphs, that swore off men, following her. Apollo is the god of poetry, music, healing, archery, diseases, the sun, prophecy and more.

Hermes

Hermes is another child of Zeus and a titan, born in hiding. As a baby he stole some of Apollo's sacred cattle but deceived him to go unpunished by creating the lyre. He's the god of travellers, thieves, merchants, roads, language and messages and soul guide to the afterlife. He is also the fastest and wittiest of the gods.

Dionysus

Dionysus isn’t special because he was born to Zeus and a human, but because his mother died before he was born Zeus stitched him into his thigh and gave birth to him that way. Unsure of his godhood Dionysus travelled the world until he found his way to Olympus where Hestia gave up her seat for him which is why there’s 5 goddesses and 7 gods, and he sits on the goddesses’ side. He is the god of wine, festivity, ecstasy and madness and is known to be very feminine.

Persephone

Persephone is Demeter and Zeus‘ daughter and goddess of spring. When Hades kidnapped her to be his wife in the underworld Demeter did not let anything grow until Persephone was returned. However, because she had eaten pomegranate seeds in the underworld, she could not return. In the end she spends half her time above ground and half below, which is why summer and winter exist because Demeter doesn’t let anything grow when she is gone.

Asclepius

Asclepius is the human son of Apollo and the god of medicine. He found a cure to bring back people from death which is why Zeus killed him. However, Apollo pleaded with his father and Asclepius was made a god but locked away to never revive people again.
